NKA DIOKA FESTIVAL

Culture is dynamic, it evolves and goes through constant modifications that is why Nkadioka cultural festival at Neni held at the heritage site called Umudioka cultural Heritage center is a must visit if you are in Anambra State, Anaocha local government Area, Neni town. Heritage sites comes along with history from the ancestors of the people, which wears around it the resilience and creativity of the people, which is called “NKA”[Artistry]. What people do, is that they try to preserve what they met and thinker through modernity on how to improve it. That was the case with the popular Nka-dioka cultural festival at Neni. Interestingly, one of the modification is the MMANKA festival, which later became known as Nka-Dioka festival in 1997. Some of other aspect non-material and material culture have been introduced- Igbo-language debate, Nka-Dioka Quiz, dance competitions and museums, Library to connect more young people to African Literature by de-emphasizing profit-making and including panel discussions Ichi and the age old practice of Igbu Ichi well known in almost all corners of Igbo land. The beginning of ichi season is heralded once annually by a special style and well recognized cry of a bird from its habitat, OFIAUDO-OJIDIKE usually in the first week of the November. The bird is known in the town as EKWULO- with the bird signaling the start of Igbu Ichi season, the Nwa Diok a sets out on his travel well equipped and in company of his lieutenants, the NWA MGBADO ICHI, NWANSA ICHI, NWANSO, etc.
